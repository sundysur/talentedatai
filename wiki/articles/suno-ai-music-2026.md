---

## What Suno Does

- Text prompt → complete, produced song (vocals, melody, lyrics, instrumentation) in ~30 seconds
- Free tier available; paid plans add generations/day, commercial rights, higher audio quality
- Output quality: radio-adjacent for established genres (lo-fi, country pop, ambient); can fall apart on complex/emotional content
- Closest analogy: what Midjourney did for visual art

---

## Who Is Using It

- **Educators:** Custom songs for classroom content (history ballads, concept summaries)
- **Indie game devs / video producers:** Bespoke royalty-free tracks matching specific mood/tempo
- **Musicians:** As compositional raw material to sample, rearrange, or react against
- **Casual users (fastest growing):** Birthday songs, joke songs, lullabies — low-stakes, joyful

---

## Hard Questions (Addressed Honestly)

- **Training data ethics:** Trained on recorded music; artists didn't consent; not compensated. Active legal disputes unresolved.
- **Economic harm:** Session musicians, jingle writers, library composers are most immediately at risk — not famous artists.
- **What it doesn't threaten:** Music as live human experience; artists you follow because of who they are.

---

## Key Insight

- "Genre of a person with something to say, saying it in song" is not under threat.
- "Functional background audio for a product demo" largely is.
- These are very different things, and conflating them generates heat rather than light.

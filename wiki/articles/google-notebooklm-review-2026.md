## Summary

Honest review of NotebookLM, a document-grounded AI research tool (not a general chatbot). Answers questions exclusively from uploaded sources with inline citations. Key feature: Audio Overview generates a 10–20 minute podcast-style discussion from any uploaded document set. Free tier is generous and the tool is genuinely differentiating.

---

## What It Is (And Isn't)

- **Is:** A private AI that knows only the documents you upload. Source-grounded answers with citations. Research companion.
- **Is NOT:** A general AI assistant. Cannot answer questions about the world. No internet access.
- **Best analogy:** An analyst who has read every document you gave them — and only those documents.

---

## Key Features

- **Source upload types:** PDFs, Google Docs, web pages, YouTube transcripts, audio files
- **Citations:** Every statement links to a numbered source in the interface
- **Audio Overview:** Generates a podcast-style discussion between 2 AI hosts from uploaded documents — 10–20 min. Uncanny quality; grounded to sources.
- **Cross-document Q&A:** "What did these 3 papers say about methodology?" across all uploaded sources simultaneously

---

## Free Tier Limits

- Up to 100 notebooks
- Up to 50 sources per notebook
- Individual sources up to 500,000 words
- Audio Overview generation included free

---

## Best Suited For

- Students and researchers (literature reviews, study guides, exam prep)
- Professionals working with long documents (contracts, policy docs, client briefs)
- Writers and journalists (cross-referencing interview transcripts and notes)

---

## Limitations

- No internet access — sources must be manually uploaded and kept current
- Audio Overviews aren't customisable (can't focus on specific sections)
- Not a replacement for deep reading; best for accelerating first-pass comprehension

---

## Getting Started

1. Go to notebooklm.google.com → Sign in with Google
2. New notebook → Upload source
3. Ask specific questions (not "summarise everything")
4. Try Audio Overview button

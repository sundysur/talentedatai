## Summary

Comprehensive review of Perplexity AI — an answer engine that searches the live web in real time and synthesises results with cited sources. 10M daily active users as of early 2026. Clear verdict: best research tool for factual questions; not a writing assistant. Covers free tier, Pro value, limitations, and a framework for when to use Perplexity vs. ChatGPT vs. Google.

---

## What Perplexity Does Better Than Google

- **Direct answers** (not a list of links)
- **Cited sources** on every claim — numbered, clickable
- **Follow-up questions** maintain full context (conversational search)
- **Academic focus mode** (Pro) — searches peer-reviewed papers
- **Real-time data** with attribution (stocks, scores, news)

---

## Pricing

| Tier | Price | Key Features |
|---|---|---|
| Free | $0 | Unlimited Quick searches; 5 Pro searches/day |
| Pro | $20/month | Unlimited Pro searches; GPT-4o/Claude model choice; file+image uploads; all focus modes |

*Tip: Start with free tier — 5 daily Pro searches may be enough for casual users.*

---

## Focus Modes (Pro)

Academic, YouTube, Reddit, News, Wolfram Alpha

---

## When to Use Each Tool

| Use Case | Tool |
|---|---|
| Factual answer with verifiable source | **Perplexity** |
| Research synthesis, current data | **Perplexity** |
| Writing, drafting, creative work | **ChatGPT or Claude** |
| Brainstorming, reasoning partnership | **ChatGPT or Claude** |
| Finding a specific website/local results | **Google** |

---

## Limitations

- Still hallucinates despite citations — verify numerical data
- Not built for generative/creative work
- Source quality inconsistent (sometimes cites low-authority blogs)
- No persistent memory on free tier
- Cannot take actions (agent capability)

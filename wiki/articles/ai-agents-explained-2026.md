## Summary

A jargon-free explainer distinguishing AI chatbots (which respond) from AI agents (which act). Uses the analogy of a knowledgeable friend vs. a capable personal assistant. Covers what agents can currently do reliably—research, coding, productivity, automation—and what they still can't do (high-stakes unsupervised decisions). Ends with a practical tip for trying agentic behaviour in Claude Projects.

---

## Key Concepts

- **Chatbot vs. Agent:** Chatbots answer per-query; agents pursue goals across multi-step workflows autonomously.
- **Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) vs. Human-on-the-Loop (HOTL):** The shift from managing each step to overseeing the process.
- **Tool use + planning = agents:** The two capabilities that made agents practical in 2025–26.
- **What works now:** Research agents (Perplexity), coding agents (Copilot, Cursor), productivity agents (Microsoft Copilot, Google Workspace), automation agents (Zapier AI, Make).
- **Current limits:** Agents still make mistakes; high-autonomy use requires human review for high-stakes tasks. Treat as "junior staff on their first week."

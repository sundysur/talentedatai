## Summary

Empathetic beginner's guide that addresses the psychological barrier ("everyone else already gets it") before teaching tools. Recommends starting with one real personal task. Provides three first-week tasks and one essential mindset shift. Lists what to safely ignore (model internals, future-of-work discourse, jargon). Three free-to-start tools: ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude.

---

## Key Approach

The article intentionally starts with the *feeling* of being left behind, not with tools. The core message: AI tools are forgiving, you cannot break them, and the worst outcome is a non-helpful answer.

---

## First Steps

1. Create a free account at chat.openai.com or gemini.google.com
2. Use it for something real from your own life (not an abstract test prompt)
3. Three tasks to try in week one:
   - Help write a difficult message (awkward email, professional follow-up)
   - Explain something confusing (lease clause, medical term, news story)
   - Summarise a long article

---

## The One Mindset Shift

Specific prompts outperform vague prompts — think of giving instructions to a smart intern. "Write an email" → "Write a brief, professional email to my supplier letting them know our order will be two weeks late, apologising without admitting fault."

---

## What to Ignore

- How LLMs technically work
- "Future of work" discourse and job replacement fears  
- Which model is "the best" — pick one, spend a month, then branch out
- Technical jargon: tokens, temperature, RAG

---

## Three Free Tools

| Tool | Best For |
|---|---|
| **ChatGPT** (chat.openai.com) | General tasks, writing, questions |
| **Google Gemini** (gemini.google.com) | Google ecosystem, research tasks |
| **Claude** (claude.ai) | Long documents, nuanced writing |

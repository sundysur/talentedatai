## Summary

A six-step repeatable content marketing workflow designed for the 2026 landscape where 60% of Google searches end without a click and AI Overviews appear for nearly every informational keyword. Introduces Generative Engine Optimisation (GEO) alongside traditional SEO. Covers from topic research through publishing and repurposing at scale.

---

## 2026 Landscape Context

- 60% of Google searches now end without a click
- AI Overviews appear for ~99.9% of informational queries
- Brands cited in AI Overviews see downstream increases in branded search
- 45% of informational AI citations → long-form articles; 41% of commercial → listicles/comparisons

---

## The Six-Step Workflow

| Step | Description | Key Tools |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Topic Research | Build topic clusters, not isolated articles | Perplexity, ChatGPT, Semrush/Ahrefs |
| 2. Content Brief | 15-min brief saves hours of editing; defines keyword, intent, outline, gaps | Claude, ChatGPT |
| 3. First Draft | Claude for long-form (2,000+ words); ChatGPT for short-form/iteration | Claude, ChatGPT |
| 4. SEO + GEO Optimisation | Traditional SEO + Generative Engine Optimisation for AI citation | Semrush, Clearscope, Claude |
| 5. Fact-Check + Original Insight | Every number verified; add one thing AI couldn't have written | Perplexity |
| 6. Repurposing at Scale | 1 article → LinkedIn post, X thread, email, video script, FAQ page | Claude, ChatGPT, InVideo, Pictory, Buffer |

---

## Generative Engine Optimisation (GEO) Principles

- Use clear, descriptive subheadings (not clever ones)
- Define key terms inline
- Include a TL;DR near the top
- Write direct answers to questions your subheadings imply
- Cite specific sources for statistics
- Shorter paragraphs preferred by AI models and human readers

---

## Key Concepts

- **Brief = strategy:** The brief is where human strategy meets AI execution; without it, AI output is generic.
- **Hallucination risk:** AI drafts will confidently state wrong statistics — Perplexity for fact verification is non-negotiable.
- **Original insight differentiator:** If your article could have been written by anyone with the same AI tools, it will compete with everyone.
- **5 common mistakes:** Publishing unedited drafts, targeting keywords without checking AI Overview competition, skipping the brief, ignoring repurposing, over-relying on one tool.

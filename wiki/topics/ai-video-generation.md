## Three Categories (Converging in 2026)

1. **Text-to-video generators:** Google Veo 3, Kling, Runway, Sora 2, Seedance
2. **Avatar/presenter platforms:** HeyGen (multilingual), Synthesia (L&D)
3. **Hybrid editing platforms:** CapCut (integrates Veo 3.1 + Sora 2), Pika

*Key 2026 trend: These categories are dissolving — avatar platforms add generative engines; editing platforms embed foundation models.*

---

## Decision Guide

- **Cinematic B-roll / photorealism:** Kling 3.0 (quality leader)
- **Narrative / story arc:** Sora 2
- **Filmmaker / VFX / camera control:** Runway 4.5
- **Corporate training:** Synthesia
- **Multilingual business video:** HeyGen
- **Social media creator:** CapCut
- **Free, high quality:** Google Veo 3
- **Budget YouTube:** Seedance 2.0
- **Open-source / unlimited:** Wan 2.6 (needs 16GB VRAM)
- **Editing existing footage:** Pika

## YouTube Automation Workflow

Claude (script) → ElevenLabs (voiceover) → Descript/InVideo (edit + captions) → Midjourney (thumbnail)

Path to monetisation: 1,000 subscribers + 4,000 watch hours for YouTube Partner Programme. Timeline: 3–6 months consistent publishing in high-CPM niches (finance, productivity).

---

## The Biggest Trend

In 2025 the question was: "Can it generate video?" In 2026 the question is: "Can you control what it generates?" Tools that answer the control question well (Kling, Runway, Sora 2) are pulling ahead.

## What AI Has Actually Changed

**Three genuine shifts:**
1. **Speed:** Tasks that took hours now take minutes — more clients/output in same time
2. **Skill floor:** Solo operators can produce professional copy, basic design, functional code without specialists
3. **New income categories:** Prompt packs, AI tutoring, no-code SaaS, AI-generated templates — didn't exist commercially 3 years ago

**What hasn't changed:**
- AI doesn't make money on its own — it amplifies skills, effort, and judgment
- Generic AI output is everywhere and buyers discount it — quality still differentiates
- The fundamental desire to work with, learn from, and be in relationships with humans

## What Expertise Actually Is (From human-expertise-age-of-ai)

Expertise = information + judgment + integrated attention + accountability + relationship

- AI has commoditised the **information** component
- **Judgment, attention, accountability, relationship** remain human
- The paradox: AI makes expertise more accessible to non-experts while making genuine expertise *more* valuable

**The real risk:** Delegating not just tasks but judgment — until the human in the loop is mostly approving outputs they can't evaluate. "The appearance of expertise on a foundation that isn't there."

## The Job Search in 2026

AI helps with the mechanical hardest parts — the blank page, tailoring, interview prep — but can't:
- Make you more qualified than you are
- Do the emotional work of searching (waiting, self-doubt, rejection)
- Replace authentic voice and real experience in interviews

## Economic Risks to Specific Groups

- **Session musicians/jingle writers** — displaced by AI music (Suno)
- **Stock photo creators** — AI image generators now accepted by Adobe Stock, Shutterstock
- **Junior writers** — commoditised by AI writing tools; premium still on voice + insight
- **Library music composers** — AI video tools include music generation

ONE THING TO TRY

If you're using Perplexity (or any AI search tool) for research, try adding "cite specific numbers, dates, and named sources only — skip general claims" to the end of your prompt. It filters out the vague summaries that AI research tools default to and forces the output toward concrete, verifiable facts. Pair it with a follow-up prompt: "Which of those claims are you least confident about?" Most tools will flag the shakier facts, which is exactly what you want to know before you use the information anywhere that matters. Takes 30 extra seconds and saves a lot of fact-checking downstream.
